Leukemia

As a highly prevalent cancer, the definitive diagnosis of acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) requires
invasive, expensive, and time-consuming diagnostic tests. ALL diagnosis using peripheral blood smear
(PBS) images plays a vital role in the initial cancer screening from non-cancer cases. The examination of
these PBS images by laboratory users is riddled with problems such as diagnostic error because the non-
specific nature of ALL signs and symptoms often leads to misdiagnosis.
